## How to Use This Checklist

**Step 1: Print or Download**
You'll want this handy as you walk through your business processes. Digital or paper—whatever works for you.

**Step 2: Go Department by Department**
Block out 15-30 minutes per department. Talk to the people doing the work—they know where the pain points are.

**Step 3: Mark Your Opportunities**
Use this simple rating system:

- ✅ **Already automated** - Great! Move on.
- 🟡 **Partially automated** - There's room for improvement.
- 🔴 **Fully manual** - This is an automation opportunity.
- ⚪ **Not applicable** - Skip it if it doesn't apply to your business.

**Step 4: Score Your Potential**
Count your 🔴 (fully manual) items. Each represents a potential automation project.

- **0-5 red flags:** You're already ahead of the curve. Focus on optimization.
- **6-15 red flags:** Typical for most growing businesses. Pick the top 3 that cause the most pain.
- **16+ red flags:** High automation potential. Start with quick wins to build momentum.

**Step 5: Prioritize**
For each 🔴 item, ask:

- How much time does this waste per week? (Hours saved = ROI)
- How often do errors happen here? (Quality improvement)
- Is this blocking growth? (Scalability impact)

**Step 6: Act**
You don't need to automate everything at once. Start with one process that will make the biggest difference. Get a win. Build from there.

## The Checklist

### 1. Sales & Marketing Automation

The lifeblood of your business. If your sales team is spending more time on data entry than talking to prospects, you're leaving money on the table.

#### Lead Management

- [ ] **Lead capture from website forms** - Do form submissions automatically flow into your CRM, or is someone copy-pasting?
- [ ] **Lead qualification scoring** - Are leads automatically scored based on behavior (email opens, page visits, company size)?
- [ ] **Lead assignment to sales reps** - Does your system auto-assign leads based on territory, specialty, or round-robin?
- [ ] **Lead nurture email sequences** - When someone downloads a resource, do they automatically enter a nurture campaign?

#### Sales Process

- [ ] **Meeting scheduling** - Can prospects book time directly on your calendar without back-and-forth emails?
- [ ] **Proposal generation** - Do you have templates that auto-populate with client details, pricing, and terms?
- [ ] **Contract creation and e-signatures** - Can contracts be generated and signed electronically without printing?
- [ ] **Follow-up reminders** - Does your CRM automatically remind reps to follow up with prospects?

#### Marketing Campaigns

- [ ] **Email campaign triggers** - Are campaigns triggered by user actions (signup, purchase, inactivity)?
- [ ] **Social media posting** - Do you schedule and auto-post content across platforms?
- [ ] **Campaign performance tracking** - Is ROI automatically calculated and reported?
- [ ] **A/B testing and optimization** - Are email subject lines, landing pages, or ads automatically tested?

**💡 Real Example:** A B2B SaaS company we worked with was manually assigning 200+ leads per month. We implemented automated lead scoring and routing. Result: Sales team closed 30% more deals because they focused on hot leads first.

---

### 2. Customer Service & Support

Your support team shouldn't be answering the same questions 100 times. That's what automation is for.

#### Ticket Management

- [ ] **Ticket creation from emails** - Do customer emails automatically create support tickets?
- [ ] **Ticket categorization and tagging** - Are tickets auto-tagged by topic (billing, technical, feature request)?
- [ ] **Priority assignment** - Do VIP customers or urgent issues get automatically escalated?
- [ ] **Ticket routing to specialists** - Are technical issues routed to tech support, billing to finance, etc.?

#### Customer Communication

- [ ] **Auto-responses for common questions** - Do customers get instant answers to FAQs?
- [ ] **Chatbot for 24/7 support** - Can customers get help outside business hours?
- [ ] **Order status updates** - Do customers receive automatic notifications (order confirmed, shipped, delivered)?
- [ ] **Proactive issue alerts** - If there's a service outage, do customers get notified before they contact you?

#### Knowledge Management

- [ ] **Self-service knowledge base** - Can customers find answers without contacting support?
- [ ] **Automated article suggestions** - When customers start typing a ticket, do related articles pop up?
- [ ] **Customer satisfaction surveys** - After a ticket closes, is feedback automatically requested?

**💡 Real Example:** An e-commerce client had support agents manually answering "Where's my order?" all day. We added automated tracking updates. Support ticket volume dropped 40% overnight.

### 3. Operations & Fulfillment

Whether you're shipping physical products or delivering services, operations is where efficiency lives or dies.

#### Order Processing

- [ ] **Order confirmation emails** - Do customers get instant confirmation when they order?
- [ ] **Inventory updates** - When an order is placed, does inventory auto-decrement?
- [ ] **Supplier reorder triggers** - When stock hits a threshold, are suppliers automatically notified?
- [ ] **Fulfillment instructions** - Do orders automatically route to the right warehouse or team?

#### Workflow Automation

- [ ] **Approval workflows** - Do purchase orders, expense reports, or contracts follow automated approval chains?
- [ ] **Task assignment** - When a project starts, are tasks auto-assigned to team members?
- [ ] **Deadline reminders** - Do team members get automatic reminders before deadlines?
- [ ] **Handoff notifications** - When one team finishes, is the next team automatically notified?

#### Quality Control

- [ ] **Automated testing/validation** - Are products or deliverables checked against quality standards automatically?
- [ ] **Error logging and alerts** - When something goes wrong, are the right people notified immediately?
- [ ] **Performance monitoring** - Are you tracking metrics like order accuracy, delivery time, or defect rates automatically?

**💡 Real Example:** A logistics company was manually coordinating handoffs between warehouse, shipping, and customer service. We built an automated workflow system. Their order processing time dropped from 2 days to 4 hours.

---

### 4. Finance & Accounting

Time is money, and nowhere is that more literal than in finance. Manual data entry in accounting is just burning cash.

#### Invoicing & Payments

- [ ] **Invoice generation** - Are invoices auto-created when work is completed or products ship?
- [ ] **Payment reminders** - Do customers get automatic reminders for overdue invoices?
- [ ] **Payment processing** - Can customers pay online via credit card, ACH, or digital wallets?
- [ ] **Receipt delivery** - Do customers automatically receive receipts after payment?

#### Expense Management

- [ ] **Expense report submission** - Can employees submit expenses via app with photo receipts?
- [ ] **Expense approval workflow** - Do expenses route to managers for approval automatically?
- [ ] **Reimbursement processing** - Are approved expenses paid out automatically?
- [ ] **Expense categorization** - Are expenses auto-tagged to the right GL codes or projects?

#### Financial Reporting

- [ ] **Revenue tracking** - Is revenue automatically calculated and reported in real-time?
- [ ] **Profit & loss statements** - Are P&L reports auto-generated monthly or quarterly?
- [ ] **Cash flow forecasting** - Does your system predict future cash positions based on receivables/payables?
- [ ] **Tax calculation and filing** - Are sales taxes calculated automatically? Do you have automated tax filing?

**💡 Real Example:** A professional services firm was spending 8 hours per week creating client invoices. We integrated their project management tool with their accounting software. Invoicing became a one-click process.

### 5. Human Resources & Talent Management

Your people are your most valuable asset. Don't waste their time (or HR's time) on paperwork.

#### Recruiting & Onboarding

- [ ] **Job posting distribution** - Do job openings auto-post to LinkedIn, Indeed, and other boards?
- [ ] **Resume screening** - Are applicants auto-filtered based on keywords, experience, or qualifications?
- [ ] **Interview scheduling** - Can candidates book interview slots without email tennis?
- [ ] **Offer letter generation** - Are offer letters auto-generated with compensation details?
- [ ] **New hire onboarding tasks** - Do new employees get automatic checklists (IT setup, paperwork, training)?

#### Employee Management

- [ ] **Time tracking** - Are hours automatically logged via time-tracking software or timesheets?
- [ ] **PTO request and approval** - Can employees request time off and get manager approval digitally?
- [ ] **Performance review reminders** - Are managers reminded when it's time for employee reviews?
- [ ] **Benefits enrollment** - Can employees select and update benefits online?

#### Payroll

- [ ] **Timesheet submission** - Do timesheets automatically feed into payroll systems?
- [ ] **Payroll calculation** - Are salaries, bonuses, taxes, and deductions calculated automatically?
- [ ] **Direct deposit** - Are employees paid via automated direct deposit?
- [ ] **Pay stub distribution** - Do employees automatically receive digital pay stubs?

**💡 Real Example:** A 50-person startup was manually tracking PTO in a shared spreadsheet. Chaos ensued. We implemented an automated PTO system. HR saved 5 hours per week and employees got instant approval visibility.

### 6. IT & Infrastructure

Your technology should work for you, not against you. Automate the boring IT stuff.

#### System Monitoring

- [ ] **Server/app uptime monitoring** - Are you automatically alerted if a server or service goes down?
- [ ] **Performance monitoring** - Are you tracking CPU, memory, disk usage, and getting alerts before issues?
- [ ] **Security monitoring** - Do you get alerts for unauthorized access attempts or security threats?
- [ ] **Backup automation** - Are databases and files backed up automatically on a schedule?

#### User Management

- [ ] **User provisioning** - When someone joins, are accounts (email, Slack, tools) created automatically?
- [ ] **User deprovisioning** - When someone leaves, are all accesses revoked immediately?
- [ ] **Password resets** - Can users reset passwords via self-service without calling IT?
- [ ] **Access management** - Are permissions auto-assigned based on role or department?

#### Software Updates

- [ ] **Security patches** - Are critical security updates applied automatically?
- [ ] **Dependency updates** - Are libraries and frameworks kept up-to-date?
- [ ] **License management** - Do you get alerts before software licenses expire?

**💡 Real Example:** An agency had no backup automation. When their server crashed, they lost 2 weeks of work. After implementing automated daily backups, they slept better. So did we.

### 7. Data & Analytics

Data is only valuable if you can act on it. Manual reporting is where insights go to die.

#### Data Collection

- [ ] **Website analytics tracking** - Are user behaviors (page views, clicks, conversions) tracked automatically?
- [ ] **Product usage tracking** - For software products, are feature usage and engagement tracked?
- [ ] **Customer feedback collection** - Are surveys or NPS scores collected automatically?
- [ ] **Sales performance tracking** - Are deals, pipeline, and revenue metrics captured in real-time?

#### Reporting & Dashboards

- [ ] **Automated daily/weekly reports** - Do key stakeholders receive reports via email automatically?
- [ ] **Real-time dashboards** - Can executives see business metrics (revenue, users, support tickets) live?
- [ ] **Custom alert thresholds** - Do you get notified when metrics hit critical levels (low inventory, high churn)?
- [ ] **Data visualization** - Are complex data sets automatically turned into charts and graphs?

#### Data Integration

- [ ] **CRM ↔ Marketing automation** - Do your sales and marketing tools share data automatically?
- [ ] **E-commerce ↔ Accounting** - Do sales automatically sync with accounting software?
- [ ] **Support ↔ Product analytics** - Can support see what customers did before they had an issue?

**💡 Real Example:** A SaaS company was manually compiling weekly revenue reports from 4 different tools. It took 3 hours every Monday. We built a unified dashboard. Now they see everything in real-time.

### 8. Communication & Collaboration

Meetings and emails shouldn't be your full-time job.

#### Internal Communication

- [ ] **Project updates** - When tasks are completed, are team members notified automatically?
- [ ] **Status reporting** - Do managers get automated updates on project progress?
- [ ] **Document version control** - Are documents automatically versioned to prevent overwrites?
- [ ] **Meeting notes and action items** - Are meetings transcribed and action items assigned automatically?

#### External Communication

- [ ] **Client status updates** - Do clients receive automatic progress reports?
- [ ] **Vendor communication** - Are purchase orders and invoices sent/received electronically?
- [ ] **Partner integrations** - Can partners access project status or inventory data via API?

#### Notification Management

- [ ] **Notification routing** - Do urgent notifications go to Slack/SMS while non-urgent go to email?
- [ ] **Digest emails** - Instead of 50 emails, do you get one daily summary?
- [ ] **Escalation workflows** - If a message isn't responded to in X hours, does it escalate?

**💡 Real Example:** A consulting firm had clients constantly asking "What's the status of my project?" We built automated weekly client reports. Client emails dropped 60%. Account managers got their lives back.

## Real Talk: Why Most Automation Projects Fail

We've seen businesses spend $50K on automation that never gets used. Here's why that happens—and how to avoid it:

**Mistake #1: Automating Bad Processes**
Automation doesn't fix broken workflows—it makes them faster. If your manual process is inefficient, automating it just gives you inefficiency at scale. Fix the process first, then automate.

**Mistake #2: Over-Engineering**
You don't need a custom-built enterprise system for everything. Sometimes a Zapier workflow or a Google Sheet with scripts solves 80% of the problem in 1/10th the time.

**Mistake #3: Ignoring the Humans**
The fanciest automation is useless if your team won't use it. Involve the people doing the work early. Get their buy-in. Train them properly.

**Mistake #4: No Clear Success Metrics**
"Automate customer service" is vague. "Reduce average ticket response time from 4 hours to 30 minutes" is measurable. Define success before you start building.

**Mistake #5: Trying to Automate Everything at Once**
Start small. Get a win. Build confidence. Then tackle the next one. Trying to automate 20 processes simultaneously is a recipe for chaos.
